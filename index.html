SHIKOKU 1889

A Game of Investment and Railroad Operation in Meiji-era Japan

Overview Setup Stock Market Operating Round Phases Glossary

Game Overview

Shikoku 1889 is part of the 18xx system, where players take on the role of investors aiming to become the wealthiest by starting, operating, and investing in railroad Corporations[cite: 1390, 1391].

Goal of the Game

The winner is the player with the highest net worth, which includes cash, share values, and Private Company face values[cite: 2550, 2551].

The Stock Market

The Stock Market tracks Corporation values. Stock tokens move based on corporate actions[cite: 1449, 1450]:

  • Up: Moves up one space if the Corporation is "Sold Out" at the end of a Stock Round[cite: 1931, 1933].
  • Down: Moves down one space for each share sold[cite: 1935].
  • Right: Moves right if the Corporation pays dividends[cite: 1937].
  • Left: Moves left if the Corporation withholds earnings[cite: 1940].

Operating Round (OR)

Corporations act in descending stock price order[cite: 2236]. Steps include:

  1. Lay/Upgrade Track: Place yellow, green, or brown tiles[cite: 2237, 2249].
  2. Place Station Token: Establish presence for ¥40[cite: 2238, 2320].
  3. Run Trains: Connect cities and towns to earn revenue[cite: 2239, 2328].
  4. Distribute Earnings: Pay dividends to shareholders or withhold to the treasury[cite: 2240, 2374].
  5. Purchase Trains: Upgrade technology before older models "rust"[cite: 2240, 2451].

Glossary

Float: When 50% of shares are sold, the Corporation receives its capital and can operate[cite: 2717].
Rust: When newer trains make older models obsolete, removing them from play[cite: 2732].
Sold Out: When 100% of a Corporation's shares are held by players[cite: 2733].
